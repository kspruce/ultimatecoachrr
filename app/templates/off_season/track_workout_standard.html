Standardized Track Workout Plan

Overview

A comprehensive 16-week progressive track workout plan designed to build speed, power, and endurance for ultimate frisbee players. Each week builds upon the previous to systematically develop your athletic capabilities.

Intervals Phase (Weeks 0-4)

Week 0 Intervals
Workout: 4 x (2min on, 2min active recovery)
Focus: Building aerobic base and teaching your body to work harder than it thinks it can.
  • On Pace: Fast run (not sprint) that challenges you but is maintainable
  • Active Recovery: Slow jog - no walking allowed
  • Goal: Maintain consistent pace across all intervals
Week 1 Intervals
Workout: 4 x (2min on, 2min active recovery)
Focus: Same as Week 0 - establish consistency before increasing intensity
  • Progression: Focus on maintaining pace throughout
  • Recovery: Active jog recovery between efforts
  • Mental: Learn to push through perceived limits
Week 2 Intervals
Workout: 6 x (90s on, 90s active recovery)
Focus: Increased volume with shorter, more intense efforts
  • Change: Shorter work periods allow for increased intensity
  • Volume: More total work time (9min vs 8min)
  • Recovery: Reduced recovery time increases challenge
Week 3 Intervals
Workout: 6 x (90s on, 90s active recovery)
Focus: Continue building aerobic base and mental toughness
  • Consistency: Maintain effort across all 6 intervals
  • Pace: Push slightly faster than previous weeks
  • Recovery: Active recovery remains crucial
Week 4 Intervals
Workout: 6 x (90s on, 90s active recovery)
Focus: Final week of interval base - prepare for next phase
  • Peak: Maximum effort for this phase
  • Technique: Focus on maintaining form under fatigue
  • Transition: Ready for speed-focused work next phase

Walk-Jog-Run Phase (Weeks 5-7)

Week 5 Walk-Jog-Run
Workout: 7 x (walk 50m, jog 200m, run 100m)
Focus: Transition to faster running with technique focus
  • Walk: Recovery between efforts
  • Jog: Active recovery - slow, controlled pace
  • Run: Fast run/sprint - faster than interval pace
  • Technique: Good upright posture, high knees, relaxed shoulders
Week 6 Walk-Jog-Run
Workout: 8 x (walk 50m, jog 200m, run 100m)
Focus: Increased volume while maintaining speed
  • Progression: One additional repetition
  • Intensity: Maintain or increase run pace
  • Recovery: Full recovery during walk segments
Week 7 Walk-Jog-Run
Workout: 9 x (walk 50m, jog 200m, run 100m)
Focus: Peak volume for this phase - prepare for hills
  • Volume: Maximum repetitions for this phase
  • Speed: Focus on maintaining top-end speed
  • Transition: Ready for power-focused hill work

Hills Phase (Weeks 8-11)

Hill Running Description

Hill sprints develop power that can be converted to speed. Find a moderate hill (20-30 seconds to run up).

Week 8 Hills
Hill Sprints
  • Structure: 7 runs up hill (6 if needed)
  • Distance: 20 seconds of running
  • Recovery: Walk down - be ready to go again
  • Special: 4th run goes to top of hill
Week 9 Hills
Hill Sprints
  • Structure: Same as Week 8
  • Focus: Maintain power and speed
  • Technique: Drive knees, pump arms
  • Mental: Push through fatigue
Week 10 Hills
Hill Sprints
  • Structure: Same as Week 8
  • Progression: Try to reach higher point
  • Power: Focus on explosive drive
  • Recovery: Full recovery between efforts
Week 11 Hills
Hill Sprints
  • Structure: Same as Week 8
  • Peak: Maximum effort for hill phase
  • Alternative: Deadmill sprints on treadmill
  • Transition: Ready for sprint technique work

Sprints Phase (Weeks 12-15)

Sprint Technique Focus

Channel all previous work into maximum speed development. Focus on acceleration mechanics and top-end speed.

Week 12 Sprints
Accelerations
  • Structure: 2 sets of 8 accelerations
  • Distance: 20m flat straight
  • Technique: Low start, drive knees, pump arms
  • Recovery: 30s between reps, 5min between sets
Week 13 Sprints
Lead-Ins
  • Structure: 4 sets of 3 lead-ins
  • Distance: 40m total (20m build-up, 20m sprint)
  • Technique: Tall and relaxed, focus on form
  • Recovery: 2-3 minutes between sets
Week 14 Sprints
Pyramid Sprints
  • Structure: 30-80m pyramid
  • Sequence: 30,40,50,60,70,80,70,60,50,40,30m
  • Technique: Full sprint mechanics
  • Recovery: Walk back recovery
Week 15 Sprints
100m Sprints
  • Structure: 7 x 100m sprints
  • Distance: Full 100m sprints
  • Technique: Maximum speed with good form
  • Recovery: Full recovery between efforts

Training Notes & Guidelines

Weekly Structure
  • Track Work: 1-2 sessions per week
  • Recovery: 48-72 hours between track sessions
  • Complementary: Continue strength training
Progression Guidelines
  • Listen to your body: Adjust intensity as needed
  • Form first: Quality over quantity
  • Recovery: Essential for adaptation
Key Success Factors
  1. Consistency: Complete all prescribed sessions
  2. Progression: Increase intensity gradually
  3. Recovery: Allow adequate rest between sessions
  4. Form: Focus on technique over speed initially
  5. Mental: Push through perceived limits
